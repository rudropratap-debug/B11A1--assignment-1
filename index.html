Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur, economist, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate, pioneered microfinance and microcredit to alleviate global poverty. Founder of Grameen Bank, he empowered millions, especially women, through access to small, collateral-free loans, fostering entrepreneurship in underserved communities. Dr. Yunus champions the concept of social business, prioritizing social impact over profit, and advocates sustainable development. His groundbreaking efforts earned international acclaim, inspiring similar models worldwide. Dr. Yunus's vision continues to drive transformative change, making a lasting difference for marginalized populations globally.
